Q1: What are your top training priorities?
================================================================================

PRIORITY 1: AUTOMATION & RESKILLING PROGRAM
-------------------------------------------
Context: We're deploying automation across 80 warehouses over next 24 months. 
Autonomous mobile robots, automated picking systems, AI-powered route optimization.
Great for efficiency, but 2,000+ warehouse workers have jobs at risk.

Current roles disappearing:
- Manual picking/packing
- Forklift operation (being automated)
- Basic inventory management

New roles emerging:
- Robot technician/maintenance
- Automation operator
- Data analyst
- Quality control specialist

CHALLENGE: Our workforce is blue-collar, many didn't complete high school, some are immigrants
with language barriers. How do we reskill them for tech roles? Can't just fire them - ethical
obligation AND labor relations nightmare (union contracts).

Need: Comprehensive reskilling program that meets people where they are. Basic digital literacy,
technical training for robot maintenance, career pathway planning. Also need change management
- people are scared and resistant.

Timeline: URGENT. First automated warehouse goes live June 2026. Need people trained by May.


PRIORITY 2: SAFETY CULTURE TRANSFORMATION
------------------------------------------
We have safety PROBLEM. 342 OSHA recordable incidents last year (43 serious injuries, 1 fatality).
Insurance costs up $18M. Regulators threatening fines. Morally: we're hurting our people.

Root causes (from investigation):
- Production pressure overrides safety (supervisors say "go fast, we're behind")
- Inadequate training (4-hour orientation then "figure it out")
- Near-miss reporting punished rather than encouraged
- Shortcuts and workarounds become norm
- "That's how we've always done it" culture
- Language barriers (instructions in English, 40% speak Spanish/Vietnamese/Polish)

Need: Not just more safety training - need culture change. Leaders must prioritize safety over
speed. Workers must feel safe reporting hazards. Systems thinking (not blaming individuals).

This is CEO's #1 priority. Board demanded action. We MUST show improvement.


PRIORITY 3: DIGITAL ADOPTION ACROSS WORKFORCE
----------------------------------------------
We've invested $280M in technology: new WMS, TMS, real-time tracking, mobile apps, analytics.
Problem: people aren't using it properly (or at all).

Example issues:
- Drivers still using paper logs instead of mobile app
- Warehouse workers scanning wrong barcodes (causing inventory errors)
- Customer service reps can't navigate new CRM (frustrated customers)
- Managers don't understand dashboards (making decisions on gut not data)

Workforce demographics: Average age 48, many in roles 15+ years, comfortable with old systems.
Change fatigue from constant "new systems." Digital literacy varies wildly.

Need: Digital skills training, change management, ongoing support. Different approaches for
different roles (drivers need different training than office workers).


SECONDARY PRIORITIES:
- Leadership development for frontline supervisors (32% turnover in some sites)
- Consultative selling for account managers (clients want strategic partners)
- Sustainability competencies (green logistics, carbon reduction)
- Customer service excellence (NPS currently 42, industry avg 58)
- Cross-cultural communication (45 countries, communication breakdowns common)

Q3: What are unique challenges of training your workforce?
================================================================================

FRONTLINE WORKFORCE CHALLENGES:
- Language barriers: 32 languages spoken, English proficiency varies
- Education levels: Many haven't finished high school
- Digital literacy: Some have never used computer
- Shift work: 24/7 operations, hard to schedule training
- Geographic dispersion: 280 facilities across 45 countries
- High turnover: 32% annual in some locations (constantly training new people)
- Physical demands: People exhausted after shift, not receptive to training

LOGISTICAL CONSTRAINTS:
- Can't stop operations for training (continuous operations)
- Budget for backfill/overtime limited
- Technology infrastructure varies by country (some sites have poor internet)
- Time zones make live training difficult
- Travel costs prohibitive for in-person training at scale

CULTURAL FACTORS:
- Warehouse culture is action-oriented, skeptical of "classroom" training
- Previous training initiatives failed (people cynical)
- Labor-management tension in some facilities (union distrust)
- Generational differences (young workers vs. 20-year veterans)
- National culture differences in learning preferences and communication styles

================================================================================
Q4: What training approaches have you tried before? What worked/didn't?
================================================================================

WHAT DIDN'T WORK:
✗ E-learning modules (low completion, people clicked through without learning)
✗ Long classroom sessions (people fall asleep, can't apply to work)
✗ One-size-fits-all global programs (didn't account for local differences)
✗ Training divorced from work (learned concepts, didn't change behavior)
✗ English-only materials (excluded non-English speakers)

WHAT WORKED:
✓ Hands-on, practical training on actual equipment
✓ Peer-to-peer learning (experienced workers teaching new hires)
✓ Short, focused sessions (15-20 minutes)
✓ Visual demonstrations (showing, not just telling)
✓ Multi-language support
✓ Immediate application (practice right away on the job)
✓ Recognition and rewards for skill development

Q5: How will you measure training success?
================================================================================

AUTOMATION/RESKILLING:
- 80% of at-risk workers successfully transition to new roles
- Automated warehouse productivity reaches 95% of target within 3 months
- Voluntary turnover during transition <15%
- Worker confidence in using new technology increases (survey)

SAFETY:
- OSHA recordable incident rate decreases 50% (to <180 incidents annually)
- Near-miss reporting increases 200% (indicator of better culture)
- Safety audit scores improve from 72% to 90%+
- Insurance premiums stabilize or decrease
- Zero fatalities

DIGITAL ADOPTION:
- System utilization rates increase to 90%+ (currently 60-70%)
- Data accuracy improves (fewer scanning errors, complete records)
- User satisfaction with technology increases
- Productivity gains from technology realized (15% efficiency improvement target)

LEADERSHIP:
- Supervisor turnover decreases from 32% to <20%
- Employee engagement scores improve in facilities with trained leaders
- Internal promotion rate increases

BUSINESS RESULTS:
- Customer NPS improves from 42 to 60+
- Operational efficiency (cost per package) improves 12%
- Revenue retention improves to 95%+
- Employee retention improves across all facilities
