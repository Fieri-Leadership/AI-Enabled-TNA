SECTION B: CURRENT TRAINING LANDSCAPE
================================================================================

B1. What training programs currently exist in your organization?
    
    Compliance Training:
    - Annual GMP refreshers (mandatory for manufacturing)
    - GCP training for clinical staff (biannual)
    - Safety and lab protocols (quarterly)
    - These are mostly e-learning modules, completion-focused, low engagement
    
    Technical Skills:
    - Vendor-provided equipment training
    - Software training (ad-hoc, inconsistent)
    - Scientific conferences (external, limited budget)
    
    Leadership Development:
    - NONE formally structured
    - Some managers attended external programs individually
    - No consistent framework or common language

B2. What is working well with current training?
    - Compliance completion rates are high (95%+) due to mandatory nature
    - Technical equipment training from vendors is generally well-received
    - Employees appreciate external conference opportunities when available

B3. What are the major gaps or challenges?
    
    CRITICAL GAPS:
    - NO leadership pipeline or development framework
    - Scientists promoted to management without any preparation
    - Post-acquisition integration - two different company cultures not blending
    - Cross-departmental silos causing delays and conflicts
    - Inconsistent quality in documentation practices (FDA concern)
    
    ENGAGEMENT ISSUES:
    - Current e-learning is boring, checkbox exercise
    - No application of learning to actual work
    - Training feels disconnected from business objectives
    - Low manager participation in coaching/developing their teams

B4. How do you currently measure training effectiveness?
    Currently we only track:
    - Completion rates
    - Post-training quiz scores (for compliance)
    
    We DO NOT measure:
    - Behavior change
    - Business impact
    - Manager effectiveness
    - Cultural integration progress
    
    This is a huge gap we need to address.

SECTION C: TRAINING NEEDS & PRIORITIES
================================================================================

C1. What are your TOP THREE training priorities for the next 12 months?

    PRIORITY 1: LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT
    Target audience: 145 people managers across all levels (first-line to senior directors)
    Critical need: Many are first-time managers, promoted from technical roles
    Desired outcomes:
        - Establish common leadership framework and expectations
        - Build foundational skills: delegation, feedback, difficult conversations
        - Improve 1-on-1 effectiveness and team engagement
        - Reduce voluntary turnover in first 90 days under new managers (currently 18%)
    
    PRIORITY 2: CULTURAL INTEGRATION & COLLABORATION
    Target audience: All employees, but especially former NeuraPharma staff (180 people)
                    and key interface teams between R&D and Commercial
    Critical need: Two company cultures operating in parallel, creating friction
    Desired outcomes:
        - Build shared identity and values
        - Improve psychological safety and trust
        - Enhance cross-functional teamwork
        - Reduce project delays caused by poor handoffs (currently 40% of projects delayed)
    
    PRIORITY 3: COMPLIANCE & QUALITY EXCELLENCE
    Target audience: 1,100 employees in R&D, Clinical Ops, and Manufacturing
    Critical need: FDA inspection findings + need for culture of quality beyond checkbox compliance
    Desired outcomes:
        - Deepen understanding of WHY behind GMP/GCP requirements
        - Improve documentation quality and consistency
        - Build ownership and accountability for quality
        - Achieve 95%+ on next FDA audit (currently 87%)

================================================================================

E1. How would you describe your organization's learning culture?
    
    Honestly? Pretty weak right now.
    
    CURRENT STATE:
    - Training seen as "check the box" requirement, not development opportunity
    - Managers don't make time for team development (too focused on deliverables)
    - Learning & Development team under-resourced (4 people for 2,850 employees)
    - No structured onboarding beyond compliance and IT setup
    - Career development conversations are rare and inconsistent
    
    POCKETS OF STRENGTH:
    - R&D teams value scientific learning (conferences, journals)

    - Executive modeling of continuous learning
    - Manager accountability for developing their people

SECTION F: SPECIFIC LEARNING OBJECTIVES & COMPETENCIES
================================================================================

F1. For LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT, what specific skills or competencies do you need managers to develop?

    FOUNDATIONAL SKILLS (for all managers):
    - Setting clear expectations and delegating effectively
    - Giving constructive feedback (positive and developmental)
    - Having difficult conversations (performance, behavior issues)
    - Coaching mindset vs. doing the work yourself
    - Running effective 1-on-1 meetings
    - Building psychological safety in teams
    - Managing conflict constructively
    
    INTERMEDIATE SKILLS (for experienced managers):
    - Strategic thinking and prioritization
    - Influencing without authority
    - Managing across cultures and geographies
    - Building and developing high-performing teams
    - Change leadership
    - Executive presence and communication
    
    ORGANIZATIONAL LEADERSHIP (for senior leaders):
    - Vision setting and cascading strategy
    - Leading through ambiguity and complexity
    - Building organizational capability
    - Managing stakeholder dynamics
    - Enterprise thinking (beyond functional silos)

F2. For CULTURAL INTEGRATION, what behaviors or mindsets need to change?

    FROM → TO:
    
    "Us vs. Them" (VitalCure vs NeuraPharma)
        → One unified company with shared purpose
    
    "My department first"
        → Enterprise thinking and collaboration
    
    "Not my problem"
        → Shared accountability for company success
    
    "This is how we've always done it"
        → Curiosity and openness to better ways
    
    "Avoid conflict"
        → Healthy debate and constructive disagreement
    
    "Just tell me what to do"
        → Ownership and proactive problem-solving
    
    SPECIFIC INTEGRATION NEEDS:
    - Understanding and respecting different work styles
    - Building trust across former company lines
    - Aligned on decision-making processes and governance
    - Common language and frameworks for how we work

F3. For COMPLIANCE training, what level of understanding is required?

    MOVE BEYOND CHECKBOX COMPLIANCE:
    
    CURRENT STATE (insufficient):
    - Employees can pass quizzes
    - Know the rules exist
    - Complete annual training
    
    DESIRED STATE:
    - UNDERSTAND the 'why' behind requirements (patient safety, data integrity)
    - APPLY principles to novel situations, not just memorize procedures
    - SPEAK UP when they see deviations (psychological safety to raise concerns)
    - TAKE OWNERSHIP of quality, not just follow instructions
    - THINK CRITICALLY about documentation and data integrity
    
    MINDSET SHIFT NEEDED:
    "Compliance is extra work" → "Quality is how we work"
    "QA's job" → "Everyone's responsibility"
    "Cover my back" → "Protect the patient"

SECTION G: DESIRED TRAINING APPROACH & PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

G1. What training methodologies or approaches do you prefer?

    MUST-HAVES:
    ✓ Adult learning principles - respect our people's expertise
    ✓ Action learning - real problems, real application
    ✓ Practice and feedback - not just lecture
    ✓ Peer learning - leverage internal expertise
    ✓ Bite-sized and spaced - not marathon sessions
    ✓ Just-in-time resources for ongoing support
    
    AVOID:
    ✗ Death by PowerPoint
    ✗ Generic corporate training that could be for any company
    ✗ Overly academic or theoretical
    ✗ One-and-done events with no follow-up
    ✗ Passive consumption of content

G2. What role do you see for technology in the training solution?

    GOOD USES:
    - Microlearning videos (5-7 minutes) for concepts
    - Virtual cohorts for geographically distributed teams
    - Discussion forums for peer support
    - Manager toolkits and job aids (easily accessible)
    - Progress tracking and reminders
    - Pre-work and post-work assignments
    
    CONCERNS:
    - Don't just digitize bad training
    - Need human connection, especially for leadership development
    - Our LMS is terrible (we know it, planning to replace Q4 2026)
    - Tech for tech's sake - must add real value

G3. How important is customization to your company's specific context?

    EXTREMELY IMPORTANT
    
    We've tried off-the-shelf programs before - they fail because:
    - Generic examples don't resonate
    - Don't address our specific challenges
    - Feel disconnected from our work
    - One-size-fits-all doesn't account for our complexity
    
    MUST BE CUSTOMIZED TO:
    - Pharma/biotech industry context
    - Our specific cultural challenges (post-acquisition integration)
    - Our leadership framework and values (once we define them!)
    - Real scenarios from our environment
    - Our systems, processes, terminology
    
    WILLING TO PAY MORE FOR CUSTOMIZATION - it's worth it
