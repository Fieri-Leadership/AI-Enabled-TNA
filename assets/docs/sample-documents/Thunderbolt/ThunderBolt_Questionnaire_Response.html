ThunderBolt Athletics - Organizational Training Needs Questionnaire

Submitted by: Marcus Rodriguez, Chief People Officer

Date: January 18, 2026

Organization: ThunderBolt Athletics (Multi-Sport Franchise)

Section 1: Training Priorities

Q: What are your top 3 training priorities?

PRIORITY 1: LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT FOR ATHLETES-TURNED-MANAGERS

We have 45 former players/coaches in management roles who never received leadership training. They're brilliant at sports but struggle with: budgets, performance reviews, conflict resolution, business strategy. Current managers inherited coaching mindset (command-and-control) rather than modern collaborative leadership. Causing friction with business-side colleagues and younger employees.

PRIORITY 2: CULTURAL INTEGRATION (TRADITIONAL SPORTS + ESPORTS)

Acquired esports division in 2024 (200 employees, avg age 26). TOTALLY different culture from traditional sports side (avg age 42). Esports team: startup mentality, casual dress, gaming-focused, digital-first, "move fast break things". Traditional side: hierarchical, formal, "this is how sports work". Integration failing - separate lunch rooms, little collaboration, mutual disdain. CEO frustrated.

PRIORITY 3: CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE & DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION

Modern sports fans expect Netflix-level digital experience. We're still operating like it's 2010. Staff need training on: mobile apps, social media engagement, data-driven personalization, digital marketing. Also need mindset shift - from "we're a sports team" to "we're entertainment and experience company".

Q: What prompted you to seek training support NOW?

Immediate triggers:

  • Employee engagement survey disaster: 48% favorable (down from 67% two years ago)
  • High turnover in esports division: 35% in first year post-acquisition
  • Fan satisfaction scores declining: 72/100 (industry benchmark 81/100)
  • Ownership demanding 15% revenue growth - need commercial capability building
  • League-mandated DEI requirements: Must hit diversity targets or face fines
  • CEO hired from tech industry (started 6 months ago) - pushing modernization agenda

Section 2: Specific Training Needs

Q: Describe your leadership development needs in detail.

Our leadership challenges are UNIQUE to sports:

Former Athletes as Managers: Incredible competitors, terrible delegators. Used to being the star performer - struggle to empower others. Don't know how to coach business skills (only sports). Some resist "corporate BS" (their words) like planning, budgets, HR processes. Need: transition from player to leader, business acumen, letting go of control.

Generational Gaps: Senior leaders are Gen X/Boomer (traditional sports background). Younger employees are Millennials/Gen Z (expect different leadership style). Older leaders say "kids today have no work ethic". Younger employees say "leadership is out of touch". Need: generational intelligence, adaptive leadership styles.

Lack of Business Sophistication: Many leaders grew up in locker rooms, not boardrooms. Don't understand P&L, strategic planning, stakeholder management. Makes them less effective in executive meetings. Need: business fundamentals for non-business leaders.

Inclusive Leadership Gaps: Leadership 77% male, 82% white in industry where players/fans are highly diverse. Unconscious bias evident - who gets promoted, who gets listened to. League threatening penalties if we don't improve. Need: genuine inclusive leadership skills, not just compliance training.

Q: What does "cultural integration" training need to accomplish?

Right now we have TWO organizations under one roof:

Traditional Sports Side says: "Esports isn't real sports. They don't understand our history, traditions, work ethic. They show up in hoodies to important meetings. No respect for hierarchy."

Esports Side says: "Traditional side is dinosaurs. Scared of technology. Move too slow. Too many meetings about meetings. Don't get gaming culture or digital natives."

Integration Training Must:

  • Build mutual respect - each side sees value in the other's strengths
  • Find common ground - shared values around competition, excellence, fans
  • Break down stereotypes and assumptions
  • Create cross-functional project teams (force collaboration on real work)
  • Establish unified organizational culture (what's non-negotiable vs. flexible)
  • Help leaders navigate cultural differences without judgment

CEO vision: "One ThunderBolt - we combine best of traditional sports excellence with esports innovation and digital savvy." Training must bring that vision to life.

Section 4: Success Metrics

Q: How will you measure training success?

We MUST show business impact, not just training metrics:

Leadership Effectiveness:

  • 360 feedback scores improve from 6.2/10 to 8.0/10
  • Employee engagement scores increase to 70%+ (currently 48%)
  • Voluntary turnover decrease to <15% (currently 22%)
  • Internal promotion rate increases (develop vs. hire leaders externally)

Cultural Integration:

  • Cross-division collaboration index (survey question) improves 40+ points
  • Successful launch of 3 integrated product offerings (trad sports + esports)
  • Esports retention improves to <20% turnover
  • Employees can articulate shared organizational values

Business Results:

  • Fan satisfaction scores increase to 80/100 (from 72/100)
  • Digital engagement metrics up 25% (app downloads, social media)
  • Revenue growth hits 15% target
  • NPS (Net Promoter Score) improves from 32 to 50+

Section 6: Additional Context

Q: What else should we know?

Sports Industry Nuances:

  • Winning matters above all - if teams lose, morale plummets regardless of training
  • Public visibility - everything we do is scrutinized by media and fans
  • Egos and competition are part of DNA - need to channel productively
  • Seasonal business with intense peak periods (training must fit schedule)
  • Players/coaches have immense influence on culture (need their buy-in)

What Won't Work:

  • Generic corporate training (eye rolls guaranteed)
  • Theory without action - this is a "doers" organization
  • Anything that feels soft or fluffy
  • Training during season (impossible to get people's attention)

What Will Work:

  • Sports analogies and examples
  • Competitive elements (teams love competition)
  • Guest speakers from sports world
  • Fast-paced, energetic delivery
  • Clear connection to winning (team and business success)
