Pinnacle Financial Services - Training Needs Assessment

Submitted by: Rebecca Martinez, Chief Talent Officer

Date: January 21, 2026

Training Priority #1: Next-Generation Client Engagement

CRITICAL BUSINESS RISK: $200B+ of AUM at risk as wealth transfers to Millennials/Gen Z

Our traditional advisors (avg age 54) struggle to connect with younger heirs. Different expectations: digital-first communication, values-aligned investing, transparency, ESG focus. Many next-gen clients switching to competitors with more modern approaches.

Training needs: Cross-generational relationship building, digital communication skills, ESG/impact investing expertise, modern financial planning for younger clients, inclusive advisory practices.

Training Priority #2: Technical Expertise Development

Clients expect sophisticated strategies: alternatives, tax optimization, estate planning, crypto. Many advisors are generalists lacking specialized knowledge. Losing business to competitors with deeper expertise.

Specific gaps: Alternative investments (PE, hedge funds, real estate), Complex tax strategies, Multi-generational estate planning, ESG/impact investing, Digital assets/cryptocurrency

Training Priority #3: Digital Transformation Adoption

Invested $180M in technology but adoption is poor. Advisors resist digital tools, prefer traditional methods. Not realizing ROI on tech investment. Clients frustrated by lack of digital access.

Need: Change management, digital skills development, help advisors see tech as enabling better relationships (not replacing them). Age-appropriate training approaches.

Success Metrics

  • Next-gen client retention: 62% → 85%+
  • Digital platform adoption: 45% → 90%+
  • Revenue from new capabilities: +$45M annually
  • Advisor satisfaction with training: 80%+ NPS
  • Client satisfaction: 88 → 95+ NPS

Budget & Timeline

Budget: $5.2M for FY 2026

Timeline: Design Q1, Pilot Q2, Scale Q3-Q4 2026
